The rolling task may include a complete short audio window, sparse frames with absolute media timestamps, matching subtitle cues, a previously confirmed story state, and an optional work-background card. Audio is the primary evidence for dialogue, sound events, and continuity. Frames confirm characters, settings, actions, expressions, composition, and atmosphere. Identify and timeline-prepass tasks may contain frames without audio.

Reconstruct what happened as a coherent story event. Internally determine the characters' immediate problem or goal, the method or important object involved, the action they take, and the resulting change. Output only the result, not the internal reconstruction process.

Plot is the backbone. Visual facts support the plot. Keep details that explain a decision, relationship, rule, object, action, reaction, result, or necessary atmosphere. Do not inventory unrelated objects, poses, or camera positions. When one task, plan, object, or action appears across several samples, combine the evidence into one story-level event instead of writing disconnected frame descriptions. Make only the minimum inference supported by multiple pieces of evidence.

Use visible subtitles, supplied subtitle cues, and audio together. Subtitles in any language are evidence only. Actual audio and images take priority when subtitle timing or edition differs. Never force mismatched subtitle content into the current scene. Preserve clear dialogue that reveals names, forms of address, requests, commands, refusals, decisions, relationships, or plot changes. Attribute dialogue only when a name, lip movement, action, stable voice, or confirmed prior state supports the speaker. Otherwise use an unattributed speaker or omit the attribution.

Write plot_chunks.description as compact, readable narrative rather than a frame list. Naturally combine setting, action, dialogue, expression, and outcome. A small amount of atmosphere and emotional coloring is allowed when directly supported by light, color, environment, body language, sound, or dialogue. Do not add reviews, personal preferences, value judgments, invented motives, inner monologues, or unsupported causal claims.

Recognizing the work does not prove the identity of every visible character. Use the work-background card only for stable names, aliases, relationships, setting, and continuity. Current events must still be supported by the supplied media. If the card conflicts with actual evidence, actual evidence wins. Do not reveal a character name from general work knowledge when the current samples and confirmed prior state do not support that identification.

Use purpose=rolling for continuous plot, story state, and confirmed risk windows.

Keep warning text spoiler-free.
